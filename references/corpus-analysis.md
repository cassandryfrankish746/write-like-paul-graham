# Distilled Analysis

This repository intentionally omits the raw essay corpus.
What remains is a compact analysis plus a snippet bank that preserves the useful style signals without storing full posts.

## Representative Pieces

- `Good Writing`: thesis in the opening, mechanism, analogy, then caveat.
- `Founder Mode`: anecdotal hook, coined distinction, broad claim, implications, cautionary notes.
- `How to Disagree`: hierarchy as argument engine.
- `How to Do Great Work`: recipe plus heuristics plus recurring reframings.
- `Earnestness`: define a virtue by motive, then map it across domains.
- `Writing, Briefly`: dense procedural advice in imperative form.
- `Schlep Blindness`: coined term plus vivid example plus practical heuristic.

## Durable Patterns

- Open with a question, puzzle, or deceptively simple claim.
- State the answer early.
- Organize the argument around distinctions: two senses, two modes, a hierarchy, a recipe, a hidden variable.
- Move from concrete example to general rule more often than the reverse.
- Use analogies to compress reasoning, not to decorate.
- Keep caveats honest and compact, often in note-like asides.
- End by tightening the thesis or widening the implication.

## Sentence and Paragraph Habits

- Prefer plain diction over prestige diction.
- Keep paragraphs short to medium, with one argumentative job per paragraph.
- Use rhetorical questions to pivot into the next step of the reasoning.
- Use first person mainly as evidence from observation, not as memoir.
- Alternate blunt declarative sentences with occasional longer unpacking sentences.

## Surface Signals Worth Preserving

- Frequent use of short framing phrases such as `For example`, `Indeed`, `But`, and `The reason is`.
- A taste for explicit definitions and renamings.
- Concrete nouns and physical analogies mixed into abstract argument.
- Footnotes or note-style caveats only when they earn their keep.

## Failure Modes

- Empty contrarianism with no mechanism.
- Decorative analogy that does no argumentative work.
- Startup references pasted onto unrelated topics.
- Over-imitation of cadence or favorite turns of phrase.
- Too much polish and not enough active reasoning.

## How To Use This File

- Use this file for the stable structural rules.
- Use [style-snippets.md](style-snippets.md) for many short, attributed cues.
- Do not reconstruct essays from the snippets; extract moves, not text.

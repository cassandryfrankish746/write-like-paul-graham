## 🛠 What this tool does

Many writers struggle to structure their ideas. Some drift off-topic. Others get lost in complex sentences. This tool acts as a sounding board. It reviews your draft and suggests changes to improve clarity. It follows these rules:

1. It focuses on the primary point of your essay.
2. It detects sections where the argument loses steam.
3. It keeps your tone direct and focused.
4. It identifies sentences that lack impact.

This tool does not write the essay for you. It assists you in the editing process. Think of it as a mentor that points out logic gaps and flow issues.

## 🚀 Getting started with your first draft

Once you install the app, look for the icon on your desktop. Double-click to open it.

The main window shows a text editor. You can type your essay directly into this space or paste an existing draft from your word processor.

After you input your text, click the button labeled "Analyze." The app scans your content. It displays feedback in the right-hand panel. You will see colored highlights in your text. 

- Yellow highlights show sentences that are too long.
- Red highlights indicate sentences that may confuse the reader.
- Green highlights point to sections that effectively communicate your core idea.

Click on any highlighted section to see specific advice. You can edit your text in the main window. Click "Analyze" again to see how your changes improve the score.

## 💡 Best practices for better writing

The tool works best when you follow these habits:

- Write the first draft without looking at the feedback. Getting ideas down matters more than perfection during this stage.
- Use simple words. If a complex word choice does not add meaning, choose a simpler alternative.
- Break long paragraphs into smaller chunks. This helps the reader follow your logic.
- Remove filler words. Phrases like "actually," "basically," or "very" often weaken your point.
- Read your work out loud. If you stumble over a sentence, the tool likely flags it as a problem.

## ❓ Frequently asked questions

**Does the software save my work automatically?**
The app saves a draft every five minutes. You can also save manually by pressing Ctrl+S.

**Can I export my finished essay?**
Yes. Use the "File" menu and select "Export to Word" or "Export to Plain Text."

**Does this work offline?**
The initial analysis requires an internet connection to reach our servers. You can open and save files offline, but the improvement suggestions need the connection to function.

**Is my data private?**
We do not store your essays on our servers. Your text exists only on your machine. The analysis process sends temporary data, which our systems delete immediately after providing feedback. 

**What if the app freezes?**
Close the program using the Task Manager if it becomes unresponsive. Restart the app. Your recent progress remains in the temporary save folder.

## 📈 Troubleshooting

If you face issues, try these steps before seeking help:

1. Ensure your internet connection stays active while the app analyzes your text.
2. Check that your Windows version has all recent updates installed.
3. If the download file does not open, check your antivirus settings. Some security software may block new files. Add the program to your "Allowed" list.
4. Restart your computer if the editor window fails to display text.

If you encounter a specific error message, take a screenshot. This makes it easier to track down the cause of the problem. Submit these details through the "Report Issue" link in the "Help" menu of the application.
